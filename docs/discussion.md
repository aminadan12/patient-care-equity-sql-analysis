# Discussion

The findings suggest that inequality appears at multiple phases of the care pathway and in different ways for different groups.

Older patients appear especially vulnerable. They had some of the highest no-show rates and some of the highest failed payment rates, suggesting that financial and logistical barriers may overlap for this group.

The project also highlights the importance of intersectionality. Different demographic groups may face different barriers at different stages, from delayed first access to appointment attendance and payment completion.

Although the dataset allows useful pattern detection, it cannot fully explain why these differences occur. More qualitative data and more direct socioeconomic measures would provide a fuller picture of patient experience and healthcare access barriers.

Overall, the project suggests that inefficiency in care delivery may reflect deeper inequities in the healthcare system.
